OVERVIEW
--------
The MTDF framework predicts H0 = 70.0 km/s/Mpc via its deterministic wavelength
lambda_MTDF = c/H0. This value lies in tension with the Planck 2018 CMB-inferred
H0 = 67.4 +/- 0.5 km/s/Mpc under standard LCDM.

MTDF includes a small Early Field Energy (EFE) contribution near matter-radiation
equality. The EFE amplitude is fixed by the late-time parameters through a
first-principles derivation:

  f_kick = lambda_MTDF / 24 ≈ 0.0033 (0.33%)

This produces a modest sound horizon shift of Δr_s/r_s ≈ -0.07%.

================================================================================
1. MTDF EARLY FIELD ENERGY: FIRST-PRINCIPLES PREDICTION
================================================================================

The MTDF stress tensor field contributes energy density during the radiation era.
At the kick epoch near matter-radiation equality, this adds fractional energy:

  MTDF prediction: f_kick = λ_MTDF / 24 ≈ 0.0033 (0.33%)

  where λ_MTDF = (1 - β_eos)² / (1 + α) ≈ 0.079

The factor 24 comes from γ_int = 1/12 (three geometric suppressions):
  - 1/3: tensor-to-scalar projection of stress field
  - 1/2: incomplete transition response at equality
  - 1/2: sound horizon time-averaging

This reduces the sound horizon by:

  Δr_s / r_s ≈ -0.07%  (shrinks r_d by about 0.1 Mpc)

In the ΔN_eff parameterization, this corresponds to:

  ΔN_eff ≈ 0.02  (at the low-amplitude end of EDE-type corrections)

================================================================================
2. CLASS VALIDATION: MTDF+EFE vs ΛCDM
================================================================================

A separate CLASS Boltzmann code analysis with full Planck TTTEEE + DESI BAO
likelihood shows that the MTDF EFE amplitude is compatible with CMB data:

  Model            k_f       H0        r_d (Mpc)    χ² (Total)    Δχ²

RESULT: Best-fit k_f = 1.00 ± 0.08 (consistent with theory prediction k_f = 1)
        Δχ² ≈ +1.25 relative to ΛCDM (essentially equivalent for 1 extra parameter)

f_EDE ~ 0.03–0.05 around equality behaves like an effective ΔN_eff ~ 0.2.
This is the regime where one can shift H₀ all the way to ≈ 70 km/s/Mpc.

MTDF's own prediction (f_kick ≈ 0.0033) corresponds to ΔN_eff ≈ 0.02, which

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

The MTDF framework includes a small Early Field Energy contribution that is
PREDICTED by theory, not fitted to CMB data:

  1. EFE amplitude is fixed: f_kick = λ_MTDF / 24 ≈ 0.33%
  2. Sound horizon effect is modest: Δr_s/r_s ≈ -0.07%
  3. CLASS + Planck + DESI confirms: k_f ≈ 1.0 ± 0.1 (theory prediction works)
  4. Δχ² ≈ +1.25 vs ΛCDM: MTDF+EFE is statistically equivalent to ΛCDM

The EFE correction moves the acoustic scale in the right direction but does not
by itself remove the distance prior tension when H0 is fixed at 70 km/s/Mpc.
The EFE amplitude is best viewed as a genuine MTDF prediction rather than a
tuned fit.
